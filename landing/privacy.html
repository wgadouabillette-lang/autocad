Lyte

Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 15, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Lyte (“Lyte”, “we”, “us”) collects, uses, and shares information when you use the Lyte website, web application, desktop application, and related services (collectively, the “Service”).

By using the Service, you agree to this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, do not use the Service.

1. Who we are

Lyte is a collaborative workspace product that includes voice channels, messaging, AI assistance, calendar and connector features, screen recording, and optional CAD/design tools. For privacy inquiries, contact us at privacy@lyte.app.

2. Information we collect

2.1 Account and profile

  • Email address and authentication method (email link, Google, Microsoft, or Facebook)
  • Display name and profile photo
  • Firebase user identifier and session tokens
  • Subscription plan, billing flags, and on-demand usage preferences

2.2 Workspace and collaboration data

  • Workspaces you create or join, membership role, and invitations
  • Presence and activity status in voice channels
  • Voice channel participation, hand-raise and knock requests
  • Direct and group chat messages between friends and workspace members
  • Polls, notifications, and join-request workflows

2.3 Content you create

  • Chat conversations, manual notes, AI Notes transcripts, and follow-up drafts
  • Screen and audio recordings you choose to capture (metadata in our cloud database; media files may be stored locally on your device in IndexedDB)
  • CAD documents, drawings, project snapshots, and related chat context
  • Calendar events and connector interactions when you use integrations (Google Calendar, Gmail, Outlook, Spotify)

2.4 AI features (Pro plan)

When you use AI chat, AI Notes, follow-ups, or vision/CAD assistance, we process the prompts, conversation history, and attachments you submit. This content may be sent to third-party model providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI) as described in our Subprocessors list. You may also store your own API keys for these providers in your private account settings; keys are stored server-side and are not exposed to other users.

2.5 Voice and video

Real-time audio and video during calls are transmitted using WebRTC, primarily peer-to-peer. We use signaling data (stored in Firebase) to establish connections. Media may traverse STUN/TURN relay servers when direct connectivity is not possible. We do not record calls unless you explicitly start recording or enable AI Notes capture.

2.6 Device and usage data

  • Browser or desktop app type, operating system, and language
  • Selected audio input/output devices and related preferences
  • Theme, panel layout, and other in-app preferences
  • IP address, timestamps, and diagnostic logs needed to secure and operate the Service
  • Local storage, session storage, and IndexedDB entries required for offline features and recordings

2.7 Payment information

Paid subscriptions are processed by Stripe. We receive customer IDs, subscription status, and billing metadata from Stripe. We do not store full payment card numbers.

3. How we use information

We use information to:

  • Provide, maintain, and improve the Service
  • Authenticate users and enforce workspace permissions
  • Enable voice, chat, calendar, connector, and AI features you request
  • Process subscriptions and usage-based billing
  • Send transactional messages (e.g. sign-in links, security notices)
  • Detect abuse, fraud, and security incidents
  • Comply with legal obligations

We do not sell your personal information. We do not use your private workspace content to train public AI models unless we clearly ask for and receive your consent for a specific program.

4. Legal bases (EEA/UK users)

Where GDPR or similar laws apply, we rely on:

  • Contract — to provide the Service you request
  • Legitimate interests — security, product improvement, and support, balanced against your rights
  • Consent — where required (e.g. optional connectors, marketing if offered)
  • Legal obligation — tax, accounting, and regulatory requirements

5. How we share information

We share information with:

  • Service providers listed in our Subprocessors page (hosting, auth, AI, payments, WebRTC relay)
  • Other users in your workspace or friend list, according to features you use (messages, presence, profile name/photo)
  • Integration partners when you invoke a connector (Google, Microsoft, Spotify)
  • Authorities when required by law or to protect rights, safety, and security
  • Business transfers in connection with a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, subject to continued protection

6. International transfers

We operate globally. Your data may be processed in the European Union (Firestore eur3), the United States (Cloud Functions, AI providers, Stripe), and other countries where our providers operate. We use appropriate safeguards such as standard contractual clauses where required.

7. Retention

We retain information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the Service. You may delete chat sessions, notes, and recordings from within the app where those controls exist. We may retain certain logs and billing records as required by law. When you delete your account, we delete or anonymize personal data within a reasonable period, except where retention is legally required.

8. Security

We use industry-standard measures including encrypted transport (HTTPS/TLS), Firebase security rules, access controls, and authenticated API endpoints. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure; you are responsible for safeguarding your account credentials and devices.

9. Your rights and choices

Depending on your location, you may have the right to:

  • Access, correct, or delete your personal information
  • Export your data
  • Object to or restrict certain processing
  • Withdraw consent where processing is consent-based
  • Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority

To exercise these rights, email privacy@lyte.app. We may need to verify your identity before responding.

10. Children

The Service is not directed to children under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 16. If you believe a child has provided us data, contact us and we will delete it.

11. Third-party links

The Service may link to third-party sites or services. Their privacy practices are governed by their own policies, not this one.

12. Changes

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will post the revised version with a new “Last updated” date. Material changes may also be communicated in the Service or by email where appropriate. Continued use after changes take effect constitutes acceptance.

13. Contact

Privacy: privacy@lyte.app
General support: support@lyte.app
